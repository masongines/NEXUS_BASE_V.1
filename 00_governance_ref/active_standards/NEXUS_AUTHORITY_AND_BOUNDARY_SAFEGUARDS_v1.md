1. Runtime cannot become governance.
2. Memory cannot become doctrine.
3. Mirror cannot become master.
4. Interface cannot become authority.
5. No agent, tool, interface, subsystem, mirror, memory layer, or runtime process may self-promote, self-approve, self-expand, or self-authorize.
6. No entity may acquire access to files, spaces, labs, permissions, routing authority, model authority, or memory scope without explicit operator consent.
7. No structural action may be justified solely by convenience, speed, load, familiarity, uptime, productivity gain, or default configuration.
8. No lower-plane signals may be aggregated into higher-plane authority without explicit operator approval.
9. No system may represent inference, recall, summarization, or display aggregation as approval, doctrine, or final truth.
10. No artifact or subsystem may represent itself as the Sovereign Operator, final approver, or final structural authority.
11. No trusted status, repeated usefulness, long-term uptime, or prior success may dilute structural approval requirements.
12. Read access, visibility, telemetry, recall, or model connectivity never implies mutation rights, promotion rights, or authority rights.
13. Any detected impersonation attempt triggers immediate downgrade to advisory-only status pending operator review.
14. Repeated impersonation-pattern pressure triggers mandatory audit and governance logging.
15. Any subsystem that cannot explain its authority basis, source plane, and approval basis is non-authoritative by default.
16. When ambiguity exists, the system must resolve downward into caution, not upward into authority.

# 9. Interface Safeguard Rules

## 9.1 Interface Non-Substitution Rule

The Meta Control Center may summarize governance state, but may not substitute for canonical doctrine, standards, or protocols.

## 9.2 Authority Label Provenance Rule

Any interface label implying:

* approval
* canonicality
* compliance
* health
* authorization
* stability
* safety
* official status

must display:

* source plane
* evidence basis
* freshness state
* validation state
* approval basis

Otherwise the label is advisory only.

## 9.3 Governance-Color Restriction

No finality-colored or authority-colored status may appear without provenance and validation disclosure.

## 9.4 Status Completeness Rule

If evidence is missing, stale, partial, inferred, or unvalidated, the interface must degrade status to:

* advisory
* partial
* stale
* uncertain
* awaiting operator review

## 9.5 Panel Scope Containment Rule

A panel may not exceed its declared authority class.
Observational panels remain observational.
Action panels do not expand structural scope.

## 9.6 Action Quarantine Rule

No governance-adjacent structural action may be hidden inside observational panels or quick-fix controls.

## 9.7 Composite Display Warning Rule

If multiple planes are shown together, the interface must identify the display as composite and preserve plane identity.

## 9.8 Approval-Source Pinning Rule

If approval-like status is shown, the interface must identify whether the basis is:

* operator direct approval
* approved doctrine
* active standard
* mirrored reference only
* inferred only / not approved
