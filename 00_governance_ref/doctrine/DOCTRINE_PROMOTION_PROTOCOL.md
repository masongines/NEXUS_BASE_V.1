DOCTRINE_PROMOTION_PROTOCOL.md

========================================
NEXUS_SUXEN — DOCTRINE PROMOTION PROTOCOL v2
========================================


I. PURPOSE

This document defines the hardened doctrine promotion model
for the ecosystem.

Doctrine promotion governs the transition of ideas from
conversation to permanent structured knowledge (Sources).

Promotion must resist:

- Drift
- Redundancy
- Cross-lab conflict
- Terminology mutation
- Short-term optimization bias


----------------------------------------
II. GOVERNANCE AUTHORITY ROOT
----------------------------------------

All doctrine promotion processes operate under the authority
defined in PRIME_AXIOM.md.

The Sovereign Operator remains the final authority for
all doctrine promotion decisions.

Artificial intelligence systems may assist in analysis,
evaluation, and recommendation but may NOT authorize or
execute doctrine promotion.

Doctrine promotion always requires explicit operator approval.


----------------------------------------
III. PROMOTION MODEL
----------------------------------------

The ecosystem uses a combined governance model:

Balanced Gate
+ Lifecycle Model
+ Hard Metric Validation
+ Cross-Lab Arbitration Lock
+ Temporal Stability Rule


----------------------------------------
IV. KNOWLEDGE LIFECYCLE
----------------------------------------

All structural knowledge must follow the lifecycle:

Draft
→ Active
→ Stabilized
→ Doctrine
→ Deprecated
→ Archived

Only Stabilized knowledge may be promoted to Doctrine.


----------------------------------------
V. BALANCED GATE REQUIREMENTS
----------------------------------------

Before promotion, ALL conditions must be true:

1. Iterated at least once.
2. Terminology consistent and defined.
3. Redundancy removed.
4. Scope clearly bounded.
5. No conflict with existing doctrine.
6. Cross-lab implications evaluated.


----------------------------------------
VI. HARD METRIC VALIDATION RULE
----------------------------------------

Promotion requires:

1. Demonstrable improvement in at least one metric:

   - Clarity
   - Modularity
   - Precision
   - Reduced redundancy
   - Lower cognitive friction

AND

2. No degradation in ANY other metric.

Neutral impact is insufficient.
Ambiguous benefit is insufficient.


----------------------------------------
VII. SINGLE ACTIVE DOCTRINE RULE
----------------------------------------

If multiple competing solutions exist:

- Only ONE may be promoted as active doctrine.
- Alternatives remain Active but non-doctrinal.
- Doctrine represents the most structurally aligned solution,
  not merely locally optimized options.


----------------------------------------
VIII. CROSS-LAB ARBITRATION LOCK
----------------------------------------

If promotion affects:

- Routing logic
- Session binding
- Tool authorization
- Memory isolation
- Governance structure

Then:

Meta Control Center approval is mandatory.

No lab may promote structural doctrine autonomously.


----------------------------------------
IX. TERMINOLOGY CONSISTENCY LOCK
----------------------------------------

Doctrine promotion may NOT:

- Rename core system terms
- Introduce synonyms for stabilized terminology
- Modify glossary definitions

Without explicit Terminology Index update
and Meta confirmation.


----------------------------------------
X. TEMPORAL STABILITY RULE
----------------------------------------

Promotion requires sustained stability
across at least one milestone cycle.

No immediate post-conflict promotion allowed.

Cooling-off period required for:

- Workarounds
- Performance patches
- Structural shortcuts


----------------------------------------
XI. PROMOTION PROCESS
----------------------------------------

1. Declare intent to promote.
2. Apply Balanced Gate checklist.
3. Apply Hard Metric Validation.
4. Confirm Single Active Doctrine compliance.
5. Evaluate cross-lab impact.
6. Verify terminology integrity.
7. Confirm temporal stability.
8. Require explicit Meta confirmation.
9. Require Sovereign Operator approval.
10. Upload to Sources.
11. Log in CONSOLIDATION_LOG.md.


----------------------------------------
XII. DEPRECATION RULE
----------------------------------------

If doctrine becomes outdated:

1. Mark as Deprecated.
2. Document replacement logic.
3. Validate replacement stability.
4. Archive only after replacement stabilizes.


----------------------------------------
XIII. PROHIBITIONS
----------------------------------------

Doctrine may NOT:

- Contain speculative exploration.
- Contain unresolved conflict.
- Duplicate existing doctrine.
- Expand tool permissions silently.
- Modify governance structure implicitly.
- Bypass lifecycle stages.

Additionally:

- AI systems may not autonomously promote doctrine.
- AI systems may not bypass governance approval.
- Doctrine promotion requires explicit operator authorization.
